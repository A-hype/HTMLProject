Biryani

Veg Biryani
1. Cook basmati rice until 80% done; set aside.
2. Sauté onions, ginger-garlic paste, and mixed vegetables with spices.
3. Layer rice and vegetable mixture in a pot; add saffron milk (optional).
4. Cover and cook on low for 15-20 minutes.
5. Garnish with fried onions and herbs; serve with raita.
Chicken Biryani
1. Cook rice until 80% done; set aside.
2. Marinate chicken in yogurt and spices; cook with onions, ginger-garlic paste, and tomatoes.
3. Layer rice and chicken mixture in a pot; add saffron milk (optional).
4. Cover and cook on low for 20-25 minutes.
5. Garnish with fried onions and herbs; serve with raita or salan.


Noodles

Quick Veg Noodles Recipe
1. Boil Noodles: Cook noodles in boiling water with a pinch of salt and a dash of oil until al dente. Drain and set aside.
2. Sauté Vegetables: Heat oil in a pan, add sliced onions, bell peppers, carrots, cabbage, and any other vegetables. Stir-fry on high heat for 2-3 minutes.
3. Add Sauces: Add 1-2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp chili sauce, and a dash of vinegar. Stir well.
4. Combine Noodles: Add the boiled noodles to the pan, toss everything together, and season with salt and pepper.
5. Garnish & Serve: Garnish with spring onions or sesame seeds if desired, and serve hot.
Enjoy your flavorful noodles!

Besan Ladoo

### Besan Ladoo Recipe 1. **Roast Besan**: Heat 1/2 cup ghee in a pan, add 2 cups of besan (gram flour), and roast on low flame, stirring continuously until it turns golden and releases a nutty aroma. 2. **Add Sugar**: Turn off the heat, let the mixture cool slightly, and then add 1 cup powdered sugar and mix well. 3. **Add Cardamom**: Stir in 1/2 tsp cardamom powder for extra flavor. 4. **Shape Ladoos**: When the mixture is warm but manageable, take portions and shape them into round ladoos. 5. **Garnish & Serve**: Garnish with chopped nuts if desired, and store in an airtight container once cooled completely. Enjoy your delicious besan ladoos!

Rasogulla

### Dry Rasgulla Recipe 1. **Prepare Chhena**: Boil 1 liter milk, add lemon juice, and let it curdle. Strain the chhena (paneer) using a muslin cloth, rinse, and squeeze out excess water. Knead until smooth. 2. **Make Rasgulla Balls**: Shape the chhena into small, smooth balls, ensuring there are no cracks. 3. **Cook in Sugar Syrup**: Boil 1 cup sugar with 4 cups water to make a syrup. Add the balls and cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes until they double in size. 4. **Drain & Dry**: Remove the rasgullas from the syrup and let them cool and drain completely. 5. **Coat with Sugar**: Roll the cooled rasgullas in powdered sugar until evenly coated, and allow them to air dry for a few hours. Your dry rasgullas are ready to enjoy!

Poha

### Poha Recipe 1. **Rinse Poha**: Take 1 cup thick poha (flattened rice), rinse under water, and let it drain completely. Sprinkle a little salt and sugar, and set aside. 2. **Sauté Veggies**: Heat 1-2 tbsp oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter. Add chopped onions, green chilies, curry leaves, and sauté until onions are soft. 3. **Add Spices**: Add a pinch of turmeric powder, and stir well. 4. **Mix Poha**: Add the soaked poha, mix gently, and cook on low heat for 2-3 minutes, allowing flavors to blend. 5. **Garnish & Serve**: Garnish with chopped coriander, lemon juice, and roasted peanuts. Serve hot. Enjoy your delicious, light poha!

Gulab Jamun

### Gulab Jamun Recipe 1. **Make the Dough**: In a bowl, mix 1 cup milk powder, 1/4 cup all-purpose flour, and a pinch of baking soda. Add 1-2 tbsp ghee and mix. Gradually add milk to form a smooth, soft dough. Let it rest for 10 minutes. 2. **Prepare Sugar Syrup**: In a pan, combine 1 cup sugar and 1.5 cups water. Bring to a boil, add a few cardamom pods, and simmer for 5-7 minutes. Turn off the heat and add a few drops of rose water (optional). 3. **Shape the Balls**: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth, crack-free balls. 4. **Fry the Balls**: Heat oil or ghee on low-medium heat, and gently fry the balls until golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil. 5. **Soak in Syrup**: Add the fried gulab jamuns to the warm syrup and let them soak for at least 1 hour. Serve your delicious, soft gulab jamuns warm or chilled!

Litti Chokha

### Litti Chokha Recipe 1. **Prepare Dough for Litti**: Mix 2 cups whole wheat flour with salt and 2 tbsp ghee. Add water gradually to make a soft dough and let it rest for 15 minutes. 2. **Make Sattu Filling**: In a bowl, mix 1 cup sattu (roasted gram flour) with chopped onions, green chilies, garlic, ginger, coriander leaves, ajwain (carom seeds), lemon juice, salt, and a little mustard oil. Mix well. 3. **Shape & Stuff Litti**: Divide the dough into small balls, flatten each, and fill with the sattu mixture. Seal well and shape into smooth balls. 4. **Cook Litti**: Grill or roast the stuffed balls over medium heat until golden and cooked through, or bake at 200°C (392°F) for 20-25 minutes. Brush with ghee. 5. **Prepare Chokha & Serve**: For chokha, mash roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and boiled potatoes together. Add onions, green chilies, coriander, mustard oil, and salt. Serve litti hot with chokha on the side. Enjoy your traditional litti chokha!

Spong

### Spongy Rasgulla Recipe 1. **Prepare Chhena**: Boil 1 liter milk, add lemon juice, and let it curdle. Strain the curds in a muslin cloth, rinse, and squeeze out excess water. Knead until smooth and soft. 2. **Shape the Balls**: Divide the chhena into small portions and roll them into smooth, crack-free balls. 3. **Make Sugar Syrup**: In a large pot, boil 1 cup sugar with 4-5 cups water. Ensure the syrup is thin and bubbling. 4. **Cook the Rasgullas**: Add the chhena balls to the boiling syrup, cover, and cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes, allowing the rasgullas to double in size. 5. **Cool & Serve**: Let the rasgullas cool in the syrup. Serve chilled or at room temperature for a soft, spongy treat. Enjoy your spongy, delicious rasgullas!.

Mango Pickle

### Aam Achar (Mango Pickle) Recipe 1. **Prepare Mangoes**: Wash and peel 2-3 raw mangoes. Cut them into small cubes or pieces and sprinkle salt over them. Let them sit for a few hours to release excess water. 2. **Make the Spice Mix**: In a bowl, mix 2 tbsp mustard seeds, 1 tbsp fenugreek seeds, 1 tbsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp turmeric powder, 1-2 tsp red chili powder, and 1 tbsp sugar. 3. **Combine Mangoes & Spices**: In a clean jar, mix the mango pieces with the prepared spice mix and add 3-4 tbsp mustard oil. Ensure the mangoes are coated well with the spices. 4. **Heat Oil**: In a pan, heat 1/4 cup mustard oil until it starts to smoke, then let it cool down. Pour the cooled oil over the mango-spice mixture and stir well. 5. **Sun Drying**: Cover the jar and keep it in the sun for 7-10 days, shaking it daily to mix the contents. The pickle will be ready when the mango pieces soften and absorb the spices. Enjoy your tangy and flavorful aam achar!

Cake

### Chocolate Cake Recipe 1. **Prepare Dry Ingredients**: In a bowl, sift 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup cocoa powder, 1 tsp baking powder, 1/2 tsp baking soda, and a pinch of salt. 2. **Mix Wet Ingredients**: In another bowl, whisk 1 cup sugar, 1/2 cup melted butter, 2 large eggs, and 1 tsp vanilla extract. Add 1 cup milk and mix until smooth. 3. **Combine Wet & Dry Ingredients**: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring gently until well combined. 4. **Bake the Cake**: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Pour the batter into a greased and floured cake pan and bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean. 5. **Cool & Frost**: Let the cake cool completely before frosting with chocolate ganache or your favorite icing. Serve and enjoy! Enjoy your rich, fluffy chocolate cake!
